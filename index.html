My current research revolves around the use of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) for various cutting-edge applications in Chemical and Materials Sciences.

I am driven by the challenge of representing molecules and materials accurately in computational systems. This interest sparked my curiosity in applying cheminformatics to study inorganic chemistry. I aim to answer key research questions such as: How can we represent molecules and materials more accurately to enable machine learning models with higher predictive power?

Currently, I am developing a Stramlit powered app for the prediction of chemical properties using GNNs. This app will allow any chemist with no coding experience to train GNN models on their own datasets. Also, the app will allow the users to predict the properties of in-silico libraries of molecules that they may want to study in the lab. Further, the app will provide interpretability tools to understand the predictions of the models.

Projects

Project 1: GNN Error Detection Tool

Developed a tool for error detection in chemical databases using Graph Neural Networks. This tool allows chemists to upload datasets and train custom GNN models.

Project 2: GNN Explainability

Focused on increasing the interpretability of GNN models by applying GNNExplainer and ShapleyValueSampling techniques.

Project 3: Chemical Space Explorer

Developed an application to visualize chemical space using graph-based representations, helping to discover new molecules efficiently.
